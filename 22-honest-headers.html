An honest profile, not a highlight reel.

What I'm actually good at. What I'm still figuring out. What I won't take on. In that order.

What I'm actually good at

The work I do consistently well.

Working with complex, ambiguous briefs

I find the actual problem before designing around it. Enterprise SaaS and operational tools are where I operate best — where simplifying the interface isn't the goal, understanding it is.

Surgical changes on live products

I know how to scope. Working inside a large inherited file, figuring out what's current, and shipping only what needs to change is something I've done and can do again.

Designing within hard constraints

The constraint IS the design problem. When nothing can move and clarity still has to improve — that's where I work well. Not against constraints, within them.

Translating logic into visual structure

Boolean logic, nested conditions, multi-step flows — I can take something technically accurate and make it humanly readable. Not by simplifying. By structuring.

Handoff discipline

Clean files, clear annotation, component documentation. I care about what I leave behind. A system that keeps working after I've moved on is part of the deliverable.

Still figuring out

Things I'm actively learning. Not gaps — growing edges.

Leading research at scale

I've done user research and synthesis — but not at org-wide scale. I'm learning how to design research programs that other teams can participate in and trust.

Designing for AI-generated interfaces

What does good UX look like when the interface is partially generated at runtime? I'm thinking about it. I don't have strong convictions yet.

Cross-platform design systems at scale

I've contributed to and extended design systems, but I haven't built one from 0 to 1 across mobile, web, and tablet for a large product family. Something I want to do.

Won't take on

Not because I can't. Because they don't need what I do well.

Pure visual / brand design

I'm not a brand designer. I can respect and work within a brand system. I'm not the right person to create it from scratch.

High-churn, low-complexity execution roles

If the work is pixel-pushing screens with no ambiguity or constraint, I'll get bored and produce mediocre work. I need problem complexity to do my best.

Work that ignores the operational reality

Beautiful designs that can't ship, or that solve the wrong problem cleanly. I won't pretend a brief makes sense if it doesn't. That's not useful to anyone.
