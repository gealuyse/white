What didn't ship

Directions I explored and set aside — with the honest reason why.

CDP · Segment Builder

A natural language input for building conditions

Why it didn't ship

It tested well with technical users who already knew what they wanted. For marketers building segments for the first time, it created ambiguity — "did the system understand me?" was a constant uncertainty. Guided structure removed that anxiety.

"Powerful" and "clear" are often in tension. Clear won.

CDP · Segment Builder

A visual tree diagram to show nested logic groups

Why it didn't ship

Beautiful in Figma. Too abstract in practice — users with non-trivial segments (5+ conditions) couldn't map the diagram back to what they were building. Indentation and inline structure was more literal and more trusted.

Some things read as "design" in the mockup but add friction in use.

3BB Member · Migration

A full redesign of the account management section

Why it didn't ship

The scope creep would have delayed the migration by months. The existing account management UX had issues — but none of them were caused by the migration. Out of scope. A full redesign would have introduced regression risk for no migration benefit.

Good design knows when to stop.

Counter Service POS

A reorganized layout with better logical grouping

Why it didn't ship

The logical grouping made sense on paper. But cashiers had trained on the existing positions. Moving elements — even to clearly better positions — required retraining during peak season. The business case didn't justify it. Working within constraints is the design skill.

The best layout is the one they can use tomorrow, not the ideal one they'd need a week to learn.
